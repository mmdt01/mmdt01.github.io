This is a placeholder essay. Essays are where you write in a looser, more
reflective register than the research notes — fewer equations, more argument.

The predictive-processing view holds that the cortex is, at bottom, trying to
predict its own incoming signals, and that perception is what happens when
those predictions meet the world. It's a seductive frame for someone coming
from machine learning, because self-supervised prediction is exactly what our
most capable models do.

But the analogy can be lazy. Write the essay that takes it seriously *and*
names where it breaks — that tension is the interesting part, and it's the kind
of writing that makes a reader trust your judgement.
